Recent Albums

Heroes & Thieves (2007, The Inc.)
Vanessa Carlton's third album was pretty good. It was a massive flop commercially (selling 75,000 copies as of 2011), but I got it the day it came out. And I loved it. Three words: "Fools Like Me." Classic Nessa is best Nessa, and while I do love this album even today, I prefer Harmonium or Be Not Nobody. Cool sidenote: Stevie Nicks (the woman, the myth, the legend!) provided backing vocals for "The One."
Drastic Fantastic (2007, Relentless)
KT Tunstall's sophomore album, an overall solid effort but overall lacking in the intimacy and warmth that makes Tunstall's other work so brilliant. The rock listener in me loves everything about this album, from the disco-ball guitar on the front cover to the lovably power-pop-infused kiss-off that is "I Don't Want You Now." But Tunstall's other work is just so much better than this album, at least by way of comparison.
Wild Hope (2007, Firm/EMI)
Mandy Moore is now better known as "an actress who can sing," as opposed to "a singer who can act." This album is one of the first "coffee-sippin', Mac-usin', easy-livin'" albums I ever bought. Moore collaborated with a bunch of other people I like, including Steve Tannen, Deb Talan, Michelle Branch, and Rachael Yamagata. The result has aged rather well, and 19-year-old me could not get enough of this album.
Kind of Blue (1959, Columbia)
The greatest assembly of jazz talent ever committed to a recording medium. The less I say, the better, but hear this: buy this record if you don't own a copy.
